Framed
[ i ]

A frame is where content is contained, it can be compared to the roll of a designer. How something is framed can also alter the perception of its content when viewed.

In Michael Rock's Fuck Content essay , he examines the roles of design and the designer, and justifies design as a content just as substantial and influential as other kinds of content, like writing and art. I wanted to create a reader that would highlight design itself as the designer's content, which lead me to the idea of frames.
Derrida on Parergon is about viewing the frame as a parergon and how it is critical in expanding our everyday understanding of what a frame is, as well as defining its relationship with a painting.
The Horse Doesn’t Get A Credit is about the design of title sequences and how they are used as frames for movies.
